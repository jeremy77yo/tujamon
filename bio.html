jeremy

also known as tu jamon,

an interdisciplinary artist
suturing collage
and found-audio composition
into unstable narratives.

scraps of daily noise
coalesce into fragile flickering songs.

the visual counterpart is
a field of xeroxed stickers,
browser artifacts,
shredded photographs
re-scanned
until physical and digital textures blur.

jeremy asks:
how does everyday noise
become a map of feeling?
how can what’s lost in degradation
open space for intimacy,
error,
and curiosity?
